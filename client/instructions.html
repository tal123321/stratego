Startego Battle

Here's a breakdown of the basic rules of Stratego:

  • Setup
    • The game is played on a square board divided by a central lake area (usually 4 squares). Each player secretly arranges their 40 pieces on their side of the board. These pieces have different ranks, ranging from Marshal (highest) to Spy (lowest). There's also a Flag piece that must be hidden among the other pieces.
  • Movement
    • Players take turns moving one piece per turn. Pieces can move one square forward, backward, left, or right (not diagonally). The Scout is the exception - it can move any number of squares in a straight line in any direction. Pieces cannot jump over other pieces or move onto squares already occupied by friendly pieces. The lakes in the center are impassable. A piece cannot move back and forth between the same two squares in three consecutive turns.
  • Combat
    • When a red and blue piece occupy adjacent squares, they can attack. To attack, the attacker taps their piece on the defender's. Ranks are then revealed - the higher-ranked piece captures the lower-ranked one and stays on the square. If the ranks are equal, both pieces are removed from the board. The Flag cannot move and is automatically captured if attacked by any enemy piece. Winning
    The first player to capture the opponent's Flag wins the game. Alternatively, if one player loses all their movable pieces, the other player wins. There are also some optional rule variations that can add more challenge, such as "Aggressor Advantage" (attacker wins in tie rank battles) and "Silent Defense" (defender keeps their rank hidden during combat).
